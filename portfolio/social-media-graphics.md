# Social Media Graphics for B2B Audio Streaming

## Customer and objectives

The design of startups frequently consists of a collection of scattered graphics that lack cohesivity. The website was crafted by a freelancer, presentations were designed by someone from the sales team, and a business card was created by someone's daughter. As a result, graphics don’t cover all business needs and look bitty. However, mindful visual identity is crucial for rapidly growing businesses. In this project, I showcase how to transfer style from one medium to another, and to build a base for visual identity.

Muzlab is a streaming audio service for commercial spaces such as malls, stores, restaurants, etc. This brand focuses on audio marketing and providing licensed music that helps businesses influence desired customers’ behavior and create an appropriate atmosphere. They hired me as a Graphic Designer to expand their visual identity to all mediums including social media, web, sales presentations, OOH advertisement, and print.

## Decoding of Visual Identity

If you need to make your brand look cohesive but do not have the wish or resources for a full redesign, you’ll need to choose one of your designs that the style will be based on. I have chosen the website - the most complex and hard-to-change part of Muzlab's visual identity. In addition, this part was the newest. Guides or rules weren't attached to the website created by a freelancer. So firstly I needed to decode them.

There is no other way than just taking all elements and formulating principles that unite them. In my case, it was the following list of elements:

1. **Typography** - The web Designer used Montserrat font - it’s a good choice, cause this grotesque has many styles and is easy to use.
2. **Colors** - The website used black, light blue, white, accent Green, and some additional colors like blue, violet, and orange.
3. **Illustrations** included next elements:
   - **Deasturated and Clipped photos** - The artist desaturates photos to make different stock photos a cohesive look. It’s a cheap and effective solution. Clipping is a powerful technique that allows the creation of collages for any theme.
   - **Wave** - The designer used the wave graphic element to put all illustration elements together. Wave is a raster brush stroke with transparent areas and shadows on it.
   - **Emanate** - Another element making graphics deep and dynamic is emanata. It's comic book artists' tricks that help to showcase emotions.

Having this toolset I was ready to create new graphics.

## Workflow for Social Media Graphics

The original style needed some improvements. To add a design system logic, the main element of the style - The Wave. It means the image can only include the wave, to be branded. Next, I returned the color, to highlight the main object of the picture, and desaturate the backgrounds to make them secondary. Also, I have changed the raster brush stroke to vector - Vector is faster to make, easier to fix, and more repeatable. Besides, I made the wave change color depending on the environment. The last change was making emanates much bolder.

Workflow for Graphics includes the following steps:

1. Desaturation of background
2. Clipping of key objects
3. Drawing a Wave
4. Adding emanate
5. Composing with text
