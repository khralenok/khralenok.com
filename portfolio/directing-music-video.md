# Directing and Filming for Music Video

## Artist and Idea of Video Clip
*Sometimes creating of Music Video needs a significant budget and time, sometimes it needs only a few people on the same wave.*

[Model Kit](https://www.instagram.com/a_model_kit/) is a musician experimenting with mixing folk and electronic music. He saw our [project with James Lo Scott](https://khralenok.com/portfolio/3d-animation-music-video-clip) and asked me to make a music video for one of his compositions from the new EP. I liked a lot what I heard, and images instantly came to my head, so I agreed.

I didn’t want to use animation for this project. I heard many noise, scratches, and stressed air in the music, that needed to be filmed on camera. ModelKit was highly inspired by this Idea, so I started to develop the project.

## The Style

Usually, I listen to music tracks many times to catch feelings that are covered in music. Listening to "Unmoor" I sketched some pictures that came to my mind. They were about narrow dirty streets, and lonely people blending with the city crowd. I think that this feeling was inspired by the mixing of voices that sound in song with electronic music. I didn't feel these images needed a color, so I decided to film in black and white. Also, to create a noisy and dirty “between radio waves” feeling, I decide to reduce the quality of the film with some filters and textures.  I wanted to achieve a psychedelic look that would immerse viewers in trans. That is why all visual was built on multi-frame compositions, reflections, negative photography, etc.

## The Narration

The narration was built on the opposite of internal and external life, which merge into the symphony. External life is reflected in plural types of moving objects - boats, motorbikes, people. This movement referred me to nomad tribes in steppes, in the middle of nowhere. Internal life is symbolized by chaotic Brownian motion dances involving eyes, hands, and full body. The visual follows the melody and showcases a human as the idea of something pointless, or like Sartre said, with a god-shaped hole.

## Workflow

The workflow was built by intuition. We filmed all nature footage with [@makedonsky.trvl](https://www.instagram.com/makedonsky.trvl/) at one day while walking by the streets of Nha Trang, Vietnam. The weather was cloudy, and the lighting was bad - so it was exactly what I wanted for style. We just followed the map of places that want to film and filmed some footage along the way.
Later I filmed eyes and hands dancing in my hotel room. [@olezhasun](https://www.instagram.com/olezhasun/) shared a video of dance from one of his dancing practices. In post-production, I have added some effects like Interlaced video, noise texture, some text elements, etc, and montaged all clips following the beat of the composition.

## Conclusions

As I said, sometimes the creation of music videos needs only a few people on the same wave. This project proves it. This counter-cultural piece avoids every aspect of modern filming looking like an archaeological find. Something that you can find in the old house’s attic. This spirit perfectly aligns with the underground vibe of Unmoor.

I highly appreciate my friends' help on this project - [@olezhasun](https://www.instagram.com/olezhasun/) for filming his dance for me, [@makedonsky.trvl](https://www.instagram.com/makedonsky.trvl/) for making a map for our walk-in Vietnam and helping to film all clips, and [@modelkit](https://www.instagram.com/a_model_kit/) who gave me creative freedom and the opportunity to express myself in collaboration with him.

Being non-commercial, this project is still complex and insightful. I love to experiment with informal genres, cause the creative freedom that they gave have many inspirations for projects with more strict guidelines. If you make a video project and wish to make it better and spend your resources more efficiently, I would be glad to share my expertise and insights with you during my consultations.
