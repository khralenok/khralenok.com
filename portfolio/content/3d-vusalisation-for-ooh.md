# 3D Visualization for OOH Advertisement



## Difficulties and goals.

At the beginning of the COVID-19 pandemic, I worked in the OOH Advertisement Company called Reklama Project. This company was focused on small businesses such as stores, beauty salons, barbershops, bars, and cafes. All these types of businesses faced difficulties at this period and not everyone survived. Reklama Projects wanted to switch the niche from small to middle-to-big businesses to stay in the game. They had a lot of sales points and didn't stop their work even after the pandemic hit. However, our processes were not prepared for this type of client, and my task was to fix it.

## What we needed to succeed

Major companies often use tenders to choose executors for their projects. It means that some OOH Advertisement Companies compete for every project, and win one that offers the best price and service. Our manufacturing team made sure we got the best price. So my part was to create the best services.

Most middle-to-large brands already have design guidebooks and you can’t make something out of the box with their style. Competitors offering similar solutions that differ only by presentation. This is what we needed to work on.

The majority of OOH Advertisement companies have the same workflow for presenting their projects: They come to the object, take photos of it from different angles, and make a photo retouching showing where lighting signs, banners, etc. should be placed. Being fast and easy, this approach doesn't show all the beauty of lighting signages and architectural lighting.

I introduced the concept of utilizing 3D graphics to create captivating and informative visuals. The benefits of this method were clear. We made the test visualization to ensure that the necessary resources were within our budget. It helped us to define all the specifications that allowed us to make the project fast and effective.

## New OOH Advertisement Visualization Workflow

We used the corresponding level elaboration of visualization depending on the project's need and budget. To ensure that the advertisement doesn't cover obstacles we used fast visualization that needs only block out of real location. We used full visualization to show complex projects involving many advertisement constructions. Requiring more complex work with shapes, materials, and lights, full visualization allows us to show the result of the work from different angles, and closeups.

A project typically goes through the following steps:

1. Drafting all advertisement elements schematically on the photo to plan work on 3D modeling.
2. Blockouting of the building and objects around.
3. Adding all advertisement elements to blockout
4. Adding details to influence perception elements of building such as doors, windows, stairs, etc.
5. Making shots or animation to showcase important aspects of our solution to the customer.
6. Preparing pith deck for the tender.
7. Getting the project.

Requiring reasonable time, this approach helped us to make high-quality OOH Advertisement projects that stand out from competitors. This method also enhanced blueprint accuracy and eliminated issues with perspective distortion in measurements. Employing advanced 3D visualization technology demonstrated our expertise and provided customers with additional reasons to work with us.

## Conclusions

We got numerous projects that helped the Reklama Project to survive during challenging COVID-19 times using this workflow. Shifting our focus from small-scale businesses to medium-to-large ones had its pros and cons, but ultimately proved a successful strategy. This enabled us to enrich our portfolio with intricate and high-profile projects, grow our manufacturing team, and receive fantastic customer feedback.
