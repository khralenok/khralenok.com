# Visual Identity for B2B Audio Streaming

## Client and goals

Zvuk Business(ex. Muzlab) is a streaming audio service for business spaces such as malls, stores, restaurants, etc. in Russia and in Kazakhstan. This brand focuses on audio marketing and providing licensed music that helps businesses influence desired customers’ behavior and create a appropriate atmosphere. My role was to create a visual identity for them.

Developing a visual identity should consider the business goals, ways to reach them, and available resources. The established business goals were to attract new clients and to enhance sales to existing ones. Tactics were to convey the value of audio streaming for businesses and to seed the concept that Zvuk Business is the leading provider of this service. Channels are landing pages, social media, e-mail, and sales pitch decks. And finally, brand visual guidelines should fit micro-production resources including a graphic designer, and a copywriter.

![visual identity](/static/images/portfolio/illustrations/illustrations-2.jpg)

## Visual Identity

Websites, sales pitch decks, and social media creatives usually include the following elements:

- Typography
- Colors
- Images

The SB Display was the only predefined element that the parent company Zvuk provided. Guidelines, graphic materials, assets, and libraries still needed to be created.

Colors are an extremely important part of visual identity, especially if you want to stand out from competitors. Most of the audio streamings prefer a dark color scheme that looks luxurious and brutal. It may look great, but the B2B brand audience is wide and motley - the nail master and bartender as marketing personas are too different, so we had only two options - to adopt an identity to each particular niche or be neutral. According to resources, the second option looks better, so we chose the light color scheme and one accent color that would strongly associate with Zvuk Business. Music is the magic - invisible, it has great power.  The color that is highly associated with magic is violet, and we chose it as the accent color for Zvuk

Images are a great tool that can amplify a message if used properly. Using photography can be a great option, but most of the major photo stocks don’t work In Russia, and custom photo shoots are extremely expensive. So, to speak to any theme without the limitation of images, we choose to use custom illustrations. And I honestly think that it worked even better than any photo for Zvuk purposes.

![Illustrations open narration tools](/static/images/portfolio/illustrations/illustrations-4.jpg)

## Narration is a framework of Marketing Message

Illustration works better cause the narration tools that they open. People often undervalue narration in images and prefer high-quality stock photos that look lifeless, rather than pictures telling stories. But what if I say that images with stories can amplify your marketing message, and the resources that you spent for it would be worth it?

To make the message stronger using illustration, we need to understand what the plain text's weak point is. The main weakness is invisibility. The world is overloaded by information so we are blind to text if don't have a proper need. To create a need we may call natural curiosity by adding near the text an image that will provoke it. Life example - if in a crowd, between many usual people, you see a girl with blue hair, she likely grabs your attention. No matter what you think next, she got your eye. The task is to recreate this “blue hair effect” for an illustration and then convert the attention that you got into a needed action.

This is the framework of Zvuk Business visual language - a pharmacist playing a thermometer like a violin or a businessman on a broom chasing the music note - all of them are hooks for attention.
