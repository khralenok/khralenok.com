# Package Design for Energy Drink

## Project Idea and Execution Plan

The idea to make a package with my illustrations came when I watched amazing videos on [Brandon Shepard’s](https://www.youtube.com/@BrandonShep) YouTube channel. Transferring your design to real-world objects is always exciting, but producing real cans just for an experiment is unjustifiably, so I decided to model it using 3D and make a captivating presentation. To structure the presentation and work I found the [DENTSU’s Vietnam case on Behance](https://www.behance.net/gallery/85713863/CocaCola-DENTSU-VIETNAM) and deconstructed it to the list of what I will need to create:

1. Drink name and key feature 
2. Three illustrations for different flavors of the drink
3. Package design
4. 3D model of can to apply my design
5. Three animations that would showcase my design

## Product Idea and Name

I sketched some concepts and stopped on energy drinks, just cause a can of Red Bull was on my desk at this moment. I ask myself “What key problem with energy drinks?” And the answer was "sugar". So I decided to make a design for a sugar-free energy drink that wakes up your inner energy and here I get the name - “Internal Energy”. Using a common symbol of energy - thunderbolt, and some fonts, I have created the logo.

## Tools and workflow

For illustrations, I used my style inspired by anime and Disney 2D animation. The style uses flat shading without any raster effects so vector graphics would fit perfectly. My main tools were a pencil for sketches and Adobe Illustrator to vectorize artwork. 

The package was also designed in Adobe Illustrator. To minimize unnecessary work, I adopted the text of the back side from another drink and focused on the front face, where the illustration and logo are placed. For illustrations, I drew three different girls dipped in the drink. This allowed me to apply the dynamic and catchy floating effect to the hair. Each character was associated with berries that drink contained by hair and eye color.

I'm a huge fan of Blender, so I used it for 3D visualization and animation. Based on some tutorials from YouTube, I modeled the can and liquid drops, created a material that looks like aluminum, and applied my design to it.

Then, I animated all the cans in a similar way as Coca-Cola in the DENTSU project. To get realistic reflections I have used physically correct Cycles Render. All animation was cycled cause I knew that would use them as a GIF and it must be as short as possible but still keep attention long time.

## Ways to use

This project was experimental but after it, I used techniques that I learned to create animations on the Zvuk Businesses website for their custom analog player, which they use in business spaces. If you want to know more about my work at Zvuk Business, check [this case](https://khralenok.com/portfolio/rebranding).

I see a lot of potential uses in this workflow - for example, I can create catchy 3D animations of products and use them in Social Media or Target Advertisements while real products still manufacturing. Also, I can apply this workflow to showcase any graphic design applied to any product. I also think that performing complex projects that usually involve more than one person(according to credits, on the DENTSU project at least four) is a great way to enhance your knowledge of processes and enhance results when you work with a team.

## Conclusions

I extremely like artistic experimental projects that touch different spheres of graphic design and content creation. Also, I am always eager to transfer the experience that I get in experiments to real projects. Package Design is an extremely deep and inspiring sphere, so I think even if my specialty is digital content, I definitely will make some projects for packaging in the future.
