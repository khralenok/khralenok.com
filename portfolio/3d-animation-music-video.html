3D animation for Music Video

Artist and Idea of Music Video

James Lo Scott is a musician and my friend who creates amazing electronic music. He made the song 'Obeshai Mne' and asked me to create a music video for it to be posted on social media. We had previously collaborated on 'NNGSH,' so this was our second music video project together. He tasked me with translating what I hear into visuals and bringing ideas to the table.

The Narration

The song narrates the story of lovers who frequently argue but never break up, with their love only growing stronger over time. They argue due to their differences but make peace because of their deep love for each other. I compared their dynamic to that of the sun and moon, which are opposites, and used the eclipse as the apex of their relationship. James appreciated this metaphor, so we made it the core of our project. We decided to base the video on imagery associated with the song's theme, featuring James and his girlfriend. This included scenes of an eclipse, a car ride, a couple gazing at each other, an origami crane, and the monkey, which was an element of James Lo Scott's branding. By alternating between these images, we aimed to immerse the viewer in a musical trance that aligns with the calm nature of the melody.

The Style

An eclipse occurs when the sun is positioned behind the moon. Together, they create the effect of a total black circle with a visible glow around it. To maintain James’s visual identity consistency, we initially aimed to retain the style of our previous work, which utilized 2D frame-by-frame animation. I sketched a few key shots, but I realized that recreating the contour glowing effect for objects in frame-by-frame animation would be extremely challenging and not feasible within our time and resource constraints. Therefore, to preserve the contour lighting as a central idea, we opted for 3D graphics as the most efficient way to achieve this effect. However, complex 3D graphics also require significant resources. To address this, I suggested using simplified geometry and real-time rendering, which was the approach used in my [Link will appear soon]. After conducting several test shots, we decided to adopt this style for the entire project.

The Workflow

This project was experimental for both of us, as we lacked similar experience before. Therefore, it was not surprising that we encountered numerous challenges along the work. The project consisted of four main steps: storyboarding, modeling, animating, and editing. Let's delve into each one.

We approached the storyboard creation similarly to our previous project, using a series of sketches for each scene to schematically depict what would happen. While storyboarding was intended to predict timing, we found it to be rather inaccurate. In reality, the length of the footage often differed from our expectations, resulting in the need for additional scenes and the reuse of certain scenes multiple times.

Modeling presented its own challenges. The most difficult aspect was creating the characters, particularly as we transitioned from a simplified design to a more realistic one to make them better resemble James and his girlfriend, Anya. While I had some prior experience with 3D sculpting for educational purposes, I had never utilized it in a real project before. Fortunately, my experience with traditional sculpting during college proved helpful in achieving the desired quality. However, most of the models were simpler and did not require as much time to create.

Animating in 3D differed significantly from 2D animation but shared similar principles, making it relatively straightforward to create all the necessary sequences. The most challenging aspect was setting up skeletons and understanding how applied animation influenced the models themselves. While Blender 3D proved to be amazing software, certain aspects were not as intuitive. Nonetheless, we persevered, and the results were well worth it.

The montage needed to support the flow of the melody. We went through three or four versions before finding the desired rhythm and look. This process was challenging, as I personally preferred using straight transitions, while James requested the addition of some effects. However, through collaboration and the merging of our artistic visions, we achieved interesting results. I particularly enjoyed the light psychedelic flavor of the transitions we ultimately chose and how they complemented the music.

Conclusions

James was extremely happy with the result and received a lot of positive feedback from his audience on social media. We were initially concerned about potential negative feedback, as we had put in a lot of effort during the project. However, the initial comments were like a breath of fresh air. I highly appreciate the trust and cooperation that James gave me. Together, we accomplished a significant amount of work, which provided us with invaluable experience.

Creating a music video is a complex endeavor, regardless of the chosen style and technologies. Producing 2-4 minutes of animation that captures viewers' attention typically requires months of work, especially when working with a limited budget and team size. One of the most challenging aspects of such projects is completing them, as many factors can deviate from the original idea, and technical limitations can impose constraints on artistic expression.
