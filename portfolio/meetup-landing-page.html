Landing Page for iOS Meetup

Goals of the project

In the summer of 2023, my friend and ex-colleague recommended me for the position of Senior Designer at red_mad_robot - a company focused on outsourcing software development. They give test tasks to candidates, so I got the one too - to design the landing page for the IT event. I have my own rules about test tasks, and one of them is to make fixed time limits that are equal to the value of this opportunity for me. For this task, I make a limit of 12 hours - it's a high value for test but challenging to do an impressive landing page. So I made a plan of upcoming works:

  1. To decode red_mad_robot visual identity and figure out what tools I have.
  2. To develop a wireframe of a landing page and to indicate zones that I can’t make without additional tools.
  3. To develop additional tools.
  4. To turn the wireframe into a prototype of the landing page.
  5. To collect red_mad_robot's feedback and to build insights.

Decoding and enhance the Visual Identity

I had red_mad_robot’s website and brand book to decode. Reviewed them, I have defined three basic components that make the brand recognizable - pixelated typography, quicksilver abstract graphic elements, and palette including black, white, and red. My project was supposed to include them all.

"Robots" used paid fonts so I have changed them to analogs. CoFo Sans was replaced with Noto Sans as the body copy font, and Redmadrobot was replaced with SHNO Pixel Code Pro as the accident font. Looking slightly different, these fonts still fit the general style.

Quicksilvery abstract elements looked a bit useless to me in the original design - I prefer graphics that are not only decorative but also functional. To engage an audience through storytelling, I used them as one of the elements for more complex collage-style illustrations. You can find details below.

Based on my surface research, a dark color scheme is more common for event landing pages, so considering the limited time I made only a dark color scheme cause it is more common for event landings. Anyway, I have utilized all brand colors - black for a background, white for a text, and red to accent attention to the most important information.

At the same time I have developed a visitor journey map and landing page wireframe.

Visitor Journey Map and Wireframe

As shown on the flowchart our goal was to turn a website visitor into an event guest. Ignoring people who aren’t interested at all, we have two behavior scenarios:

  1. The visitor comes to the landing page, checks the hero section, clicks on CTA, fills in the participant form, and then turns into the guest.
  2. The visitor comes to the landing page, and checks the hero section, but is still not sure about participation, so he reads additional information, then scroll until the form, fills it out, and then turns into the guest.

The hero section was important for both scenarios, so we needed to make it catchy and informative enough to engage A an B groups.

Informativeness ensured by placing all significant data in each corner of hero section as on the event poster - event name, time, and place. Following this logic, I placed CTA last by the clockwise movement. To move a visitor’s eye move in this direction I used cycling animation of an object, turning around an axis.

For B-Scenario I built hypothesis about reasons why people which interests match event’s topic can reject to participate them. The first is “I’m not sure about the relevancy of speeches for me”. The second is “I’m too unqualified/experienced to participate in such events”. And third, is “It would take too much time”. Each “No” is answered by a corresponding block i.e.:

  1. Speeches - Giving extra details about speech topics, speakers, and their roles.
  2. Audience - Providing an understanding of the event's target audience.
  3. Schedule - Allowing us to understand what time the event would take.

Last but not least is the form that used to provide visitors data to organizers. It should includes fields for e-mail that would used to connect with visitors, for a name that would help to build personalized experience in future organizations e-mails , and for a participation type that separating online guests from offline.

Creating of Landing Content and Prototyping

When I got the landing page wireframe and the visitor journey map I could move to content creating.

I did the fastest things at first such as applying fonts and colors to the wireframe and making the basic layouts for each block. It allowed me to see particular requirements that graphics should meet. After this, I made a list of graphics includes animation for the hero section, three animated badges for speech cards, and an illustration for the audience section that are needed to be created.

I started with frame-by-frame pixel animation for badges which was funny to stylize. As a reference for them, I have used outlined Google Material Icons. I have recreated them using a 32x32 grid, and then made by four states for each and animated them using the component prototyping Figma feature.

Then, I moved to the animation of Quicksilver iPhone 3D model for hero section. For this part, I used free 3D model stocks to find ready-to-use model and Blender to create the material, setup the scene, and render the model.

Using the same material I created abstract elements easily in the 3D sculpting. I need them to use as a part of an illustration based on characters clipped from “The Creation of Adam” artwork for the Audience section. Plot of the illustration was inspired by the title “From Junior to Team Lead”.

The last part was to prototype all interface elements. I gave special attention to the form fields to show how they work. You can click to any field in the prototype and then press “A” to check it. Also, I have highlighted the usability of the switcher by using this element twice. The first was the animation of the schedule item hovered condition that refer to the iOS alarm app. The second was as the online/offline type of guest switcher that replaced the checkbox or drop list that are common for this need.

Feedback and Insights of the Project

I got this amazing feeling performing on this project. It was felt like a hackathon. Time frames made me optimize every process on the run to focus on the essence of the project. I like this approach very much.

I have completed the project in the estimated time and sent it to red_mad_robot for review. They promptly organized a meeting with the product and marketing team to give me feedback. They underlined how I matched their style and developed it. Also they appreciated the deep analysis and logic of the project. As a result, I got a job offer.

I really appreciated it but, I didn’t accept this offer cause another interesting project became. Anyway, I was highly inspired by the interesting test task and “Robots” team approach to feedback, so I would be happy to work with them in the future.

This project was focused on graphic design and user experience, but during my work I was interested in working on it from the marketing perspective too. Testing graphics and copywriting using social media, e-mail, and paid traffic can lead to surprising insights that would enhance results and can significantly change every solutions. So if you have an upcoming event project, I would be happy to participate it as a creative director and content strategist.
