# Illustrations for Poetry Website

## Idea and Goals of Project

I have written stories since I was a kid and some of them have rhythm and rhymes. When I was a student I posted my poems on social media, and read them at festivals and poetry evenings. But getting older I more and more tried to hide this part of myself to the box, cause so many people told me “You can’t be everything at the same time” and “It’s unserious - grow up”. Common story. But I never really stopped writing and eventually, I decided don’t hide it anymore. Even more, I wished to show it using my skills in illustration, web design, and social media. I wanted to make a webzine that includes 10 of my poems, each followed by an illustration drawn in my style, and public it on the web. So, I started from the list of what I need to do:

1. To edit my poems, to make them consistent.
2. To choose a project name.
3. To develop a visual Identity for the project.
4. To draw custom illustrations for each poem.
5. To make a webzine design.
6. To build and publish webzine on the web.
7. To tell about the webzine on social media.
8. To get emotional and practical insights.

![Poem page mockup](/static/images/portfolio/poetry-website/poem-page-mockup.jpg)

## Preparation

I took all my poems, written for the last 12 years, and chose ten that I want to share. They weren’t consistent, cause years lay between the birth of each one. To keep an essence I decided to edit them only a bit, to make them share a common theme - “Stop trying to be somebody else, be yourself”. For me, this theme is related to the feeling of putting life on until later. 

The word “later” is translated to my native Russian in the transcript as “potom”. The word “poem” in the Russian transcript sounds like a future form of the word “eat”.  I mixed it into “poempotom” - that sounds funny to me, cause it means “I will write poems later” and “I’ll eat later” at the same time. This name refers to the maniacal inspiration that artistic persons have. Condition, where we even forget to do basic things like eating or sleeping. 

Having the name of the project and the text part I was ready to move to the visual identity part.

## Visual Identity

The website aesthetic was inspired by underground print. So, I took a few qualities that underground zines have:

1. Faded-out black inks - it’s common cause cheap paper can’t take a lot of inks and you can’t get real rich black
2. Sketchy style illustrations - underground zines are always made from the stuff under hands so grungy pen-style fits extremely well to create this feeling.
3. One accent color - Webzines are often printed in cheap ways and adding only one color is a good way to show it. Like a screen-printing.

Turning cheapness into the style is challenging, but I went further. I wished to make it a digital manner, using vector graphics and clean colors. So I used graphite black, red that goes to orange and white as the color palette, and built an illustration style based on fills and strokes.

## Tools and Process

To create illustrations I used the gel pen for sketches and then vectorized them using Adobe Illustrator. Each illustration had its genre: multi-figure compositions, portraits, still life, landscapes, and multi-dimensional images. I have rendered them to .svg for the best appearance and to speed up website page loading. 

For building a website, I took outdated workflow - I wrote all the code on plain HTML and CSS without using any constructors. This approach underlined the underground spirit of the project. It was challenging not to use Webflow or another visual constructor, but I made adaptations for mobile devices and added a few special things like a feature that allows scrolling only poems but always see an Illustration or a menu that is always seen on one page without scrolling no matter what device you using. When all parts of the project came together, I needed to publish it. I bought the domain and hosting on GoDaddy. It was funny to go through all the “Make a custom website in our website builder in few clicks” banners while publishing a website built in the early 2000’s manner.

The last part was to film the video about the project on my social media. I have warmed up my audience on Instagram by publishing some process parts and between-steps results. Then I published the video where I told about the project, idea, and execution process and shared the link to my brand new webzine.

## Results and Insights

![Main Page Mockup](/static/images/portfolio/poetry-website/main-page-mockup.jpg)

Publication of my website brought positive and warm reactions from my audience. Many of my followers engaged and shared my page in their stories and also sent me their feedback in comments and directly. I got amazing organic reach for the first month of Webzine existence without using any paid traffic sources. I have not planned to continue the experiment forever and update this webzine, so monitored statistics only during the year after launch. Predictably page’s reach has reduced over time but the result that I wanted to get was already got.

On the Poempotom project, I got experience in executing complex projects, involving many different content creation disciplines. I have studied to warm up an audience to announce new projects, and I got positive feedback from the community. In the next projects, I’ll want to test managing organic conversion through the content and multiply this experience.
