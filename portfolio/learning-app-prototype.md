# Prototype of a Learning App
## The Idea of an Application

We use a lot of application everyday for various tasks. There are apps even for drop smoking or for defining what type of rock you see. Time to time Ideas of different apps come to me too. I try to be respectful to every idea. To record them and research their potential I use prototypes.

![Idea starts from simplified graphic editor screen ](/static/images/portfolio/learning-app/idea.jpg)
*Idea starts from simplified graphic editor screen*

Real life example: When I worked at Saint Petersburg School of Television as a Graphic Design Tutor, my students often had a troubles with composition. Being most fundamental part of graphic design, principles of elements organization are crucial to understand . One of the way to figure it out is release from figurative perception. Instead of this you should see every element as an abstract shape that have own weight and role in composition. I built a lot of exercises that helps to figure out this topic. Desired organize every piece of my knowledge I came up with idea of the application, that allow students to study composition in visual and interactive form.

## Visual Identity

Visual style of Application inspired by avant-garde art of early XX century and suprematism artist who worked with basic geometric forms and lines in their arts.

To highlight the retro vibe I choosed faded out color palette that feels like printed on old paper. Not intence color solution is also comfortable for long reading. Based on style I should use some block accent typography, but it was to heavy for screen usage. I choose Montserrat font as easy to read gotesk that still have poster aesthetic.

Badges and other materials was follow suprematism stylistic and looks like they have been created in same application. Icons look similar to used in every design applications that students will use when they start to make their real world projects.

## The Application Architecture

Learning app is kind of textbook, no matter how interactive it is. And any textbook always have a table of content. So first screen that you would see as a user is the home screen where you see the list of topics and your learning progress. From this screen you can easily dive to lesson.

Lesson screen have a text and supporting illustrations, pagination and ability to make bookmarks. After you finish reading, you need to secure your knowledge by practice. Button in the end of each lesson lead you to test task.

Tests are involve making of compositions following rules that lesson was about. You would get some tools such as shapes, lines to make your composition. Number of usage of each tools can be predefined by the rule of particular lesson. When you finish it, you would send your work to the tutor, and after his review, you would get a feedback.

Feedback screen include tutor comment and files where tutor show the mistake. From this screen you can easily come back to lesson to read topic again or retake the test. Also feedback page displays the grade.

Every work would be save to personal gallery. This feature would allow students to discuss their work with their group. This practice is very helpful for studying. Also personal gallery displays student achievements that give extra engagement for students, highlighting events such as “Three A-grade in row” or “New chapter”.

This Architecture can be developed deeper, but I prefer don't overwhelm prototypes on this stage. If project will developing further, things could change and over-elaboration would only interfere. Having a basic architecture I moved to visual identity for my app.

## Conclusions

For this sort of application, the main weakness is the limited customer lifetime - you can use it only during the study. It isn't bad for an online school that works with hundreds of students, but in this case, the application should cover more than one topic.

However, it's a model that can be scaled up for particular needs. I see an adaptation a bit on the side from casual learning.
For example, you can take any of the typical tasks that your Account manager does and turn them into interactive ones that would be implemented in the customer dashboard. This approach helps to delegate some work to the customer and redirect resources to make the service that you provide better. Sometimes a solution would be worth it, sometimes not.

The main point is every idea should have a chance. Use different types of prototyping to record it and see it in detail. Feel free to contact me for consultation - we will discuss your application idea and I give you a valuable review and share my best practices.
