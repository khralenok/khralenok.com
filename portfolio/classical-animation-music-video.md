# Classical Animation for Music Video

## Artist and Idea of Video Clip

James Lo Scott is the musician and my friend who creates amazing electronic music. Once we met at one of Khabarovsk's bars. He showed me the song demo, and I showed him an animation I did for fun.  This song was inspired by Afansy Fet's poem, written in 1843, the song sounds brand new in James’s reading. My animation style was supposed to support it and make it visually modern. We decide to mix them.

We both were excited to make our first music video, so I rode home imagining future shots and started drawing the same evening.

## The Style

The Visual style of our music video was inspired by my experiments with high-contrast poster graphics. It was based on the usage of 3 colors - black, white, and accent. Also, I have experimented with applying grunge textures to flat colors to achieve a more detailed look. This technique also laid in the base of our visual solutions.

Every transition between scenes was drawn frame by frame in a liquid manner. This solution was due to my lack of knowledge about montage techniques at the start of the project. I figured out how to make more classic sort of animations with straight cuts almost instantly, but we liked these liquid transitions so much, that decided to stay on them.

However, during the work, my skills grew a bit so my style of drawing also changed. The fact that it was one of my earliest projects in animator and illustrator roles also gave the project a unique DIY aesthetic.

# The Narration

The narration of our video was inspired by the original Afanasy Fet’s poem: 

> …
> Night. The city noise has faded out.
> 
> There's a star in heaven; like a spark,
> 
> In my in doleful heart it has aroused
> 
> Serious reflection, in the dark.
> …

The video showed walking by the night city. Empty streets, alone silhouettes in the windows, a man going along the railroad sleepers, cigarette butt flying away - all these elements allegorically show the loneliness of the hero who just walking around drowning in his thoughts. The flow of these thoughts is shown by different abstract elements shape-shifting from one object to another. This simple plot allows to immerse viewers in the video and gives the music an additional edge through the visuals.

# Workflow

Whole video was drawn in Photoshop using Video Layers feature.

Considering the informal nature of the project, we skipped a lot of formal procedures like approving storyboards and key shots, etc. I just sent animatics that I already drew to James for feedback from time to time. While I worked on the video, he continued his work on the song.

The animation was created in Photoshop, using their Video Layer Feature. I started with a sketch of each frame with only a few frames per second. This draft allowed me to understand the general flow of animation. Then, I drew the final version on the layer above.

This approach had its cons. The video layers function was not as good as now, plus I didn’t know many tricks and was compelled to use a very complex approach of recreating each frame from scratch. It may sound a bit strange, but I even didn’t separate background layers and characters, because my computer was ready to jumpstart to the space just because I ran the file with so a long video. So maybe if freezes were not so crazy, I would figure out how to make it “by rules”, but I didn’t.

However, technical limitations are not the reason for excuses, but one of the factors influencing artistic solutions. This approach can be called flipbook animation, but digital. I even heard about a few TV animation projects based on it.

# Conclusions

We presented our music video on the big screen in the same bar where the idea was born for all James Lo Scott fans and got amazing feedback. The unique style of this animation attracted the attention of another musician, who also wanted to make something similar. People talked about our project, which means our main goal was achieved - we grabbed attention to our art.

Fun fact James has changed the style of the song in the process of the music video creation, so the video used the demo version of this song.
