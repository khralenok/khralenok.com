# How to Become a Creative Director
## Why I Become a Creative Director

For me, this role was a result of a bunch of attempts to be somebody else. And not the last one, be honest. I typed in search field phrases [How to become a …] whenever I felt myself stressed in frames and saw some opportunity to end this feeling. 

When I was a child idea of choosing of right vector for myself sounded from every corner. Or maybe just for me, this idea was louder than others. But I always was stable and unstable at the same time. I do every work that I do as well as possible and dive deep into any field that I work with,  and someday I find myself arguing with my boss or coworker about something that is out of my responsibility field, cause everything that lies in doesn’t help to achieve the business goals. Yep, it’s who I am - a pain in the ass - I care about general success, not only about mine. 

So actually I became sort of Creative Director in every place where I worked. Sometimes not official, but… 

![qualities-of-creative-director.jpg](/static/images/blog/blog-creative-chief/qualities-of-creative-director.jpg)

## Must-have qualities for Creative Director

In June of 2023, I got a job at a marketing agency as the Creative Director. My responsibility was to build video and photo production in 5 countries for one gadget brand. I didn’t get in touch with video production before except few animation clips that I made before and some small projects that I worked on freelance. But I think that every creative process is similar. You just need a have some personal qualities to do this job.

As I said before, I did all this in my previous positions in some way. So I think the first must-have quality for a Creative Director is being bigger than any particular role that you would manage. If you see all separate fields of workflow as an integer process that leads to a strategic goal this role is for you. Usually, it is called “See the whole picture” but when every picture has a frame, at the same time chaos that you would work with is frameless.

The second quality is being extremely logical. Every creative idea needs to be described as a “We do [something] to get [something]. To avoid frustration, every participant in the creative process must understand the reasons and goals of this process. So you need always make everything as clear as possible and be ready to accept that even when you make everything elementary somebody would still don’t understand it. It looks like fighting with windmills first, but later you always understand that the reason somebody doesn’t understand you is a fact that you didn’t fully understand yourself, and when you make something clear, the main beneficiary is you.

The third quality of a Creative Director is being an accepting person. Mostly people trying to do their best divided to their current physical and mental state. So if your expectation was much higher than the real result - often it task description problem, or a misunderstanding of resources. Both are your responsibility. You would create and improve task description formulas continuously. But they would be still imperfect, so you must be a great communicator to fill the gaps in the tasks. Also, people have limitless productivity, so you need to understand these limits and respect them. Remember, you are so helpful not only cause you are a kind person, but also and mostly cause your effectiveness is a sum of the effectiveness of your team.

So do you see the big picture, can explain it logically, and accept that things often go in different ways than planned? If the answer is “Yes”, then you should try!

## So how become a Creative Director

Firstly, try yourself in a safe environment - make an independent project that involves more than one person. It would be easier if you had a bit of money to pay these people. It gives you an understanding of project planning, budget management, and a connection between your expectations and final results.

If you feel comfortable with the first project, take it to your portfolio and start to search for a job. Take two weeks to send your resume to every company that hires an art director, creative director, or somebody who manages creative teams. During this process analyze the requirements that these companies have and the responses that you get - it helps you to make insights in the future.

Maybe you find the job on the first attempt, maybe not - it’s unpredictable. If not - try again but make it analytically. Analyze your first attempt, based on insights build 3 hypotheses about why you don’t get it, solve these problems if possible, set up a new deadline, and then try again. One day you get it.

## Conclusion

I shared my experience and tried to describe it honestly and clearly. Being a Creative Director may be fun and interesting, but also hard emotionally and physically cause with great power to create something bigger than one person can do comes the great responsibility for result of project execution. Also, I want to notify you that a Creative Director is a problem solver who is involved in each process of a company and when you start your work you will face hundreds of processes that are not installed properly yet, are organized chaotically, and will interfere you to perform your work well. Part of your job would be to fix it. 

And another one, for persons who are always bigger than any role, be ready that Creative Director is not your final point. For example, during my work in the agency from the Creative Director I became the Content Strategist but it’s a different story.

I wish you luck trying yourself and great projects!
