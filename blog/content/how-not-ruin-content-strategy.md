# How Not Ruin Your Content Strategy

## What this case is about

The customer is always right - the golden rule of business. Often, people hide their incompetence under this imperative. I met many people who said “But it’s what you asked for, isn’t it?”, making excuses. The patient's responsibility is to say he has pain, and the doctor's is to know how to cure it. And sometimes when you try to satisfy a client, you do a disservice to him. Let’s look at an example.

![all-seamed-well.jpg](/static/images/blog/blog-tnt/all-seamed-well.jpg)

## All seemed well

I got an offer for the position of Art Director at the marketing agency that works with Apple Official Reseller in CIS countries. My role was to establish and supervise the production of content for social media and paid advertisements. I was supposed to provide content that would be posted on local accounts Instagram and Facebook to perform the content plan established by the SMM manager. Content is supposed to be created by local creators, whom I needed to find, hire, and supervise during their work.

To save resources, I preferred to work with ready materials that creators were happy to share for free or for a few bucks. Creators were getting an extra reach, and we were getting a content-fair deal. The only problem was that amount of ready content had a limit. So sometimes we needed to make custom shootings. When you build remote content production a lot of decisions depend on the executors - What location to choose? What actors to film? How to film each particular piece of script? So independence was the most important quality that I looking for. 

Just as an illustration: I required a few photos of murals in Almaty, to show the wide-angle lens of the iPhone. I composed a list of murals in this city and a mood board and provided them to the creator. He waited about a week for a sunny day suited for shooting, but the legendary smog over Almaty didn’t go. So when the deadline almost came and he rode to shoot,  we discovered that most of the murals marked in the list were destroyed already. Of course, we worked with what we had, and the post was published, but it's not an exception, it is how every shooting goes - quite unpredictable. But this hard work was worth it - content got impressive average ER metrics - 10% for Instagram and 4% for Facebook.

Regardless, metrics that were shared with clients were focused on boosting reach and followers and no one knew how good the content that we published was. So when the client asked to change content strategy, guided by the golden rule “Customer is always right” we did it.

![how-milk-spilled.jpg](/static/images/blog/blog-tnt/how-milk-spilled.jpg)

## How milk spilled

"Modern users only watch videos", “People want to get information faster”, and “Nobody reads nowadays” are a small part of arguments in favor of Reels. So, if they are so great, why do we just not change the strategy to post only them? That’s how our client motivated radical change of course.

For some reason, it didn't influence on budget. Videos are a complex and expensive type of content including writing scenarios, casting, location scouting, and other aspects. So, the fact that posting high-quality dynamic content costs more than static is not surprising. Film 15 videos by the budget for 12 photos and 3 videos is a challenging task, but I was up to the challenge.

Even despite new responsibilities. The SMM manager left our team and nobody came to change him, so his task lay on my shoulders. I not only supervised content creation but also made content plans to perform the inherited strategy. To make a content plan you need to know what goal it needs to serve. Our content plan must lead the traffic from social media to partner stores where people can buy gadgets. I composed the marketing funnel and corresponding content types:

1. For getting to the Awareness stage we used inspiring content - time lapses, backstages, creator interviews, .etc. The pros of this content is access to the creators' audience.
2. To wake up new subscribers' Interest, We used how-to tutorials in the mobilograhy field. People often save and share this sort of content, so it was supposed to enhance account authority.
3. To push the audience to the desired stage, We used a tech blog format that shows the advantages and features of the product.
4. The last step is Action - we used discounts and promotions that the client provided.

Working in theory, in practice, this strategy dropped ER from 10% to 1% on Instagram and from 4 to 0,5 on Facebook with no positive dynamic in the future. Hypotheses were ambiguous - someone said the acting was not so good, someone blamed filming quality, and another said scenarios were boring - these are questions of taste. By the way, metrics research didn’t show any significant difference between groups of videos sorted by any of these qualities - all worked equally badly. 

In my opinion, there were two reasons. First - the audience majority started following the account at the period when we were posting static content, that they were engaged by. We jump-shifted to the new strategy and the unprepared audience just lost interest in content different from one they subscribed for. The second reason was quality - despite our shift to video format, we still operated same budget that spent on photo before. Most of the photos were pre-created which allowed pay more for shooting that we made ourselves. So overall quality of content was much higher and dropped after the strategy was changed, just because different type of content needs different budgets to meet the same level of quality.

## Crash and burn

On another strategic session with the customer, the agency got tons of negative feedback. To fix it we promised to develop a new strategy that significantly improves the results of SMM. 

The account manager left our team and left the client's wishlist. They wanted funny reels, collaborations with influencers, special projects, engaging storytelling through the stories, and many other things. But the business goal stayed the same, they wanted to increase conversion to landing page visitors. To make it more challenging, the budget was reduced. 

Considering the resources and results that we already had, I prioritize conversion higher than their content-related wishes. I analyzed every post and saw an interesting picture. Independently was it period when we posted mostly dynamic content or static, last one worked much much better. Also, I saw a strong dependency on ER from the text of the publication so the idea that "nobody reads nowadays" was wrong. Our audience reads. So we needed just to come back to our previous strategy and improve static image impact using text tools. 

I separated static content into three groups by function based on the most effective previous posts. iPhone Photos and iPad art by local creators were supposed to inspire the audience, information about products and functions was supposed to create an interest and desire, and discounts and promotions were supposed to push the audience to action. Video content was supposed to serve the only goal - to increase the reach. So videos are supposed to use trend music and plots and be as simple as possible. The proportion of static and video content was 80% and 20% respectively.

“Client is always right”, remember. My team extracted the arguments and used them to sell the client his wishlist items as the brand new strategy. Experimental reels focused on reach became “viral reels” which are advertisements with complex acting and twisted plots. Static iPhone photos and iPad art became complex collaborations with influencers, etc. As before to satisfy the client we changed the strategy to a more complex one with unpredictable efficiency, ignoring the fact that he wanted to increase conversion and tools weren't so important.   

Felt that it wouldn’t work, so I filmed a few reels just to check myself. And… they brought the same bad results and were extremely hard to film and approve with the client. His expectations were much higher than before, and we can't do it considering resources. I spent three months under the pressure of responsibilities that lay on three people before, doing my best to improve the situation, and finally, faced my limits. When every day turned to hours of arguing with the client and team I asked myself - “Is it my war?” and every time answer was "No". 

## So How Not Ruin a Content Strategy?

I’m not a huge fan of leaving projects, especially when so many resources were spent on them, but sometimes it’s the only choice. This project gave me tons of valuable experience in building remote content production and content strategy, making analytics, and building insights on it. So answering the title question I’ll share some thoughts with you.

1. For customers: Focus on your business goals and what you want to get from any marketing activity and try to provide it to the executor as accurately as you can and he will tell you how to achieve it. It is fine to argue, be wrong, and admit that but remember If you tell the executor how to do their job - fire him better, not lose your time.
2. For Content Strategists: Be data-driven and logical, trends come and leave, and marketing stays. Build the strategy on client goals and your resources. Content is not the goal, content is the tool. And there is no magic, just consistent targeted efforts to improve content for particular needs. Discuss these needs with clients and managers until you understand them completely.
3. For Creative Directors: Be honest about your resources. Being up to challenge sometimes is equal to damping on yourself. You can get a quality product on any budget, but a product would be different. The price must be aligned with the income that this piece of content can bring. To understand this income, speak with the client, and ask about the goals of the content. Don't do things just to do anything.
4. For C-Level persons: Keep close attention to each team member's load. Overloaded employees can’t react quickly, so when you notice that something burns it can be too late.

Anyway, content strategy and SMM are very deep and complex, so If you need help to understand your particular needs and tools to achieve your goals, I would be glad to help you.
