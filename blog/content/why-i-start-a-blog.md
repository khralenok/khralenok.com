# Why I start a blog… again
## Just a blog

To answer this question I need to tell you about myself a bit. I’m an artist, graphic designer, and creative marketing manager and I am stuck in the feeling of all that I do - it’s just putting myself in frames that are so much smaller than I am… That’s why I am always trying to break these frames and start something valuable for me. So the short answer is khralenok.com -  the blog that I want to fill with my personality and give life to all my thoughts, ideas, and projects. And… It’s so complicated that I need to write the long answer too

## Everything is so tasty, but what I should choose?

I studied at the College of Arts and did sculptures, drawings, and paintings. It was extremely interesting, but at the moment of graduation I was pretty sure that it was too dangerous to put all my professional hopes on something “not digital”.  The reason for my belief in digital is my family. The two most successful members of it are programmers who moved to the USA. So I decided to make something digital but still aligned with art and actually, I did it. 

Most of my career is strongly connected with graphic design and illustration and I have done a lot of projects and worked in different environments from freelance to in-house, but always I felt invisible borders in every role that I played.

On the side, I also tried different things - writing, poetry, game development, programming, animation, videography, photography, comics, guitar but every particular activity also felt like the frames.

“And what is the deal? Ok, you are a polymath, a renaissance person and you need artistic freedom and the opportunity to express yourself. The case is closed.” - Says my inner voice(I often speak with myself in that way - to depreciate my problem) 

And I partly agree with that voice. But I also have a strong rational part that says - “You need to earn money to have an opportunity to eat what you like, wear what you want, pay rent, and all these things.” This rational part is afraid of something unstable and unknown. The idea that I can do everything that I want fears me, cause what I put into my resume at the final?

## Micro-effort concept

And even with this fear, I tried. Half of my career I build as a freelancer. I did so many different things that I still can gather it to something logical. And on this path, I was extremely anxious and trying to focus on something. But I always crashed by the frames and felt disenchantment, again and again coming to the thought that I needed to find the sense in being the jack-of-all-trades.

I still trying something new, but very accurate about risks, so I have developed my formula for success. I call it “Micro-efforts”. Yes, it’s not unique - “Micro efforts lead macro effect”. The concept is that I can achieve something in any field that I want but efforts must be small enough to be iterative as long as possible. It sort of fuses burnout in the process of goal achieving. 

For example, if want to be in good physical form I can do a sport for about 1.5 hours 3 times per week. It looks like strategy, but I have my job, girlfriend, hobbies, and home. So if some of this needs a bit more attention I just drop the next training and theт all this activity. Cause it’s the wrong strategy for me. It assumes rejection of something - framing. I need to avoid any frames. But I still want to have a strong body. So I need to find so small effort amount, that would be repeatable in the long term. For me, it is a 15-minute sports routine every morning. With some gaps, I have done it for about 4 years already, and maybe my body is not like the bodies of guys in trousers box, but I won’t weigh 100 kg as weight at 2020 - that sort of victory too.

Returning to the blog, I tried different forms of blogging many times, but every time I ended up dropping this activity for many reasons. This blog is an experiment of making blogging using the Micro-efforts tactic. Let’s see where it would lead.
