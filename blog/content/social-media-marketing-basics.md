# Social Media Marketing Basics

## Why Businesses Come to Social Media

It often seems like they come by inertia - “Everybody comes and I do” 

But it has a sense. The main feature of Social Media for businesses is access to their potential customers on these platforms. Businesses invest in advertisement on platforms so Social Media are interested in working with them. They even help to reach an audience for those who share engaging content by recommendations. The ability to get amazing reach for free turns Social Media into an owned marketing channel allowing businesses to reduce the price of new customer acquisition.

In this post, we will look through some basics and calculate metrics to form expectations about your Social Media activity.

## No Journey Without a Map

<center>Viewers/Conversion = 1/(step1Throughput*...*stepXThroughput)</center>

The journey from the first touch with your content to conversion is long. Key points of this journey forming Customer Journey Map. You can easily make yours by listing every step your customer should go through to get your product.

But be attentive to this journey length, people easily wander off track following too many steps. The formula above shows how each additional step increases the viewers you need to get one conversion. Ensure that your customers don’t go through unnecessary steps. 

## Content is the king

<center>ER = (Interactions/Views)*100</center>

Social Media Users are hungry for content, but choosy also. To craft valuable content that people will appreciate Think of your content in the way it perfume sample. You need to provide the feeling of having your product.

Whether you're sharing text, images, or videos, understanding these key metrics is crucial for content relevance:

👀 **Views** - Reflects user reach.

🫵 **Interactions** - Indicates engagement through shares, comments, and likes.

📈 **Engagement Rate (ER)** - Measures relevancy by comparing interactions to views.
